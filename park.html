Leamington's Best Park

Point Pelee National Park

When you're tired of looking at Leamington's seemingly endless acres of monotonous greenhouses, what better place to soak in natural beauty than at Point Pelee National Park. Established in 1918, the "point" was the first national park in Canada to be established for conservation. The park is a huge attraction for bird enthusiasts in the spring as tired migrants rest after their journey north across the lake. If you're lucky, you might even see an elusive turkey or two. You never know what rare flora or fauna is around the corner in Leamington's best showcase of Mother Nature.
